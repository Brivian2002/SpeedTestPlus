Understanding Results

What Do Your Speed Test Results Mean?

Decode your download, upload, ping, and jitter readings.

Download Speed

How fast data travels from the internet to your device. Important for streaming, browsing, and downloading files. Measured in Mbps (Megabits per second).

25+ Mbps = Good for most activities

Upload Speed

How fast data travels from your device to the internet. Crucial for video calls, cloud backups, live streaming, and sharing large files online.

5+ Mbps = Good for video calls

Ping (Latency)

The reaction time of your connection — how fast you get a response after sending a request. Critical for gaming, video calls, and real-time applications.

< 30 ms = Excellent for gaming

Jitter

The variation in ping over time. High jitter causes choppy video calls and lag spikes in games. Consistent, low jitter means a stable connection.

< 5 ms = Stable connection

ISPs advertise "up to" speeds, meaning maximum theoretical throughput. Actual speeds vary due to network congestion, Wi-Fi interference, distance from router, number of connected devices, time of day, and server load. Wired (Ethernet) connections typically show higher speeds than Wi-Fi.
